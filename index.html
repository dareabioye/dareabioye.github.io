Academic Formation

Deep Reinforcement Learning for Wind Farm Control: A Hyperparameter Optimisation and Interpretability Study
MSc Dissertation · Arden University · Overall: Distinction · Dissertation: 85% · 2026

A rigorous five-chapter investigation into the application of Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) for wind farm control optimisation, comparing three DRL algorithms — PPO, SAC, and TD3 — against classical baseline controllers (PI, MPC, Greedy). The study integrates systematic hyperparameter optimisation with SHAP-based explainability analysis, identifying three critical failure modes in DRL policy behaviour. Built on a Gaussian wake model simulation environment, the research combines post-positivist methodology with statistical validation and effect size calculations — contributing directly to both academic DRL research and industry-facing renewable energy systems.

My Journey

My story begins in Lagos, Nigeria — where a BSc in Statistics from the University of Ibadan set the foundation for what would become an 18-year career spanning four continents, multiple industries, and now the frontier of artificial intelligence.

From processing survey data at DC Consult to leading Nigeria's largest retail census at PSI, and then rising through Ipsos to become Head of the Pan-Africa Situation Room — a continental quality control hub — I learned early that data is only as powerful as the rigour behind it.

Seven years at Ipsos refined my ability to translate complex data into strategic decisions for regulators, central banks, fintechs, and multinational corporations. The Access to Financial Services Survey — 24,000+ respondents annually — was not just a project. It shaped Nigeria's financial inclusion policy.

In 2022 I moved to Konstanz, Germany — joining the University of Konstanz Development Research Group, co-authoring peer-reviewed publications, and completing an MSc in Data Science with Distinction (Dissertation: 85%). My dissertation explored Deep Reinforcement Learning for wind farm control — sitting at the intersection of AI and the global energy transition.

The journey continues. Open to research collaborations, PhD opportunities, and senior data science roles that push the boundaries of what data can do for the world.
